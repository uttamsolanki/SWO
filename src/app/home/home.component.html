Overview

OWC – Wastewater Treatment Plant Greenhouse Gas Emissions Calculator

Background

The Ontario Water Consortium (OWC) believes innovation is necessary to solve our local and global water challenges. These innovations become marketable solutions when private sector and academic knowledge combine in real-world environments to overcome technology commercialization challenges.

The purpose of OWC’s GHG calculator is to provide technology developers, consultants, or wastewater treatment practitioners a tool to help evaluate and compare the impact to greenhouse gas emissions in a wastewater treatment plant when new technologies or process modifications are under consideration. This information may be valuable to justify the adoption of a new technology or process, or to support applications for funding programs.

Links and/or downloads to all references are provided. Note that this is a calculation tool, not a model and the sole output is an estimated calculated value for total CO2 (equivalent) when all input fields are complete.

User Information

The design of this calculator is intuitive, user friendly, and flexible. After registering a username and password, users must first define a new “Project”. From the dashboard, the user can create a new project or see a summary of all previously created projects by that user. The user can create and compare up to four scenarios under a project. It is not possible to compare scenarios from different projects. It may be desirable that the first scenario under a project is always a “baseline” of the wastewater treatment plant under evaluation. Users can enter any value in the various input fields but should choose to either utilize the suggested values (sources, links, and downloadable documents are all provided where applicable) or input actual values from the wastewater treatment plant. Scenarios within a project can also be duplicated (and re-saved as a new scenario) in order to adjust parameters without starting from the beginning. However, existing scenarios cannot be edited once saved (available as view only).

Users will input data in three separate categories: electrical emissions, chemical emissions, and process emissions. Under the electrical emissions category, users will select the desired unit operations and processes to represent the wastewater treatment plant under evaluation. The user will either choose from the given technology options or create a custom entry for each of the liquid wastewater stream, sludge stream, biogas stream, and biosolids dewatering and disposal stream. The user will also input the power consumption on a flow basis for each unit operation selected. In the chemicals section, user inputs include the quantity of chemicals utilized at various spots and for different applications in the treatment plant. In the process emission section, user inputs include specific process values (effluent parameters, sludge production, external sludge and organics information, methane concentration, etc.) for use in the calculation. An illustration of the results for each individual scenario is in the form of multiple flow diagrams, summary tables, and summary graphs.

Remember to save your scenario, by clicking the ‘Save Scenario’ button located at the bottom of the Summary page; otherwise, your calculator entries will be lost.

Calculator-Basis

Basis of Calculations in this calculator:

  • CO2 equivalence for electricity (Ontario) due to Nuclear Power, g CO2e/kWh = 40
  • COD/TOC Ratio, g-O2/g-C = 3.33
  • COD/TOC Ratio, g-O2/g.mole-C = 32
  • N2O emitted (as % TKN removed) = 0.34

Biosolids Disposal – Basis of Calculation for biosolids disposal are:

  • Land Application – Beneficial use of biosolids results in 0 emission of CO2e and CH4.
  • Landfill without methane recovery – emitted methane from the landfill is converted to 25x CO2e per unit mass of methane and added to the rest of the carbon that is converted to CO2 by natural process.
  • Landfill with methane recovery – all the 100% of methane is converted to CO2e [1 mole of methane to one mole of CO2] and added to the rest of the carbon that is converted to CO2 by natural process.
  • Incineration – all the 100% of the carbon in the sludge is converted to CO2e [1 mole of carbon to 1 mole of CO2]
    1. Incineration within the wastewater treatment facility will not require transportation. User can deselect the transportation option.
  • Transportation of sludge is calculated independent of disposal and the user can select either transport distance or transport duration.
